Our hometown

The story of Pflugerville

So many of us moved here recently and never heard the tale of how this little German farm town on the prairie became one of the friendliest places in Texas. Here’s the short, sweet version — pull up a porch chair.

First things first

What’s with all the “Pf”?

If “Pflugerville” looks like a typo, blame — and thank — a German farmer named Henry Pfluger Sr. “Pflug” is simply the German word for plow, and the family name carried that hard-working spirit all the way from Hesse to the Texas blackland prairie.

Locals have long had fun with it. You’ll spot the proud “Pf” everywhere — from Deutschen Pfest, the city’s German heritage festival, to friendly nicknames for the schools, parks, and pfun community events. It’s a little wink that says: we know exactly who we are and where we came from.

1850s

A family, a plow, and 960 acres of prairie

Henry Pfluger was a prosperous farmer in Altenhasungen, in the German region of Hesse — until the upheaval of the Revolutions of 1848 cost him nearly everything. Like so many neighbors who’ve come to Pflugerville since, he packed up his family and set out for a fresh start, arriving in Texas around 1849–1852.

He bought a first parcel of land east of Austin from his brother-in-law, and in 1853 traded up for a 960-acre tract near a spot called Brushy Knob, along the banks of Gilleland Creek. The Pflugers were the first German family to settle and work the land here. Home was a five-room log cabin; the fields grew corn, wheat, rye, beans, sweet potatoes, and sugar cane. Life on the early prairie was hard and the country was wide open — but the family dug in and stayed.

After the Civil War, a real community began to take root. Neighbors built a school in 1872 on Henry Lisso’s farm and founded Immanuel Lutheran Church a couple of years later — the kind of institutions that turn a cluster of farms into a town.

The short version

Pflugerville through the years

  1. 1853
    Henry Pfluger Sr. moves his family onto a 960-acre farm near Gilleland Creek — the seed of the town.
  2. 1872–74
    The first school opens, soon followed by Immanuel Lutheran Church. A community is born.
  3. 1890–93
    Louis Bohls opens the first general store and the post office. Folks finally bank and ship from home instead of riding to Round Rock.
  4. 1891
    Neighbors form a mutual-aid association to insure against disaster — plus a beloved German shooting-and-bowling club, the Schützen und Kegel Verein.
  5. 1904
    The Missouri–Kansas–Texas Railroad arrives. George Pfluger and his son Albert lay out the official town site and donate land for a depot and school.
  6. 1907–42
    The town reads all about itself in The Pflugerville Press.
  7. 1958–62
    Pflugerville High School football wins 55 games in a row — earning the little town national fame.
  8. 1965
    Pflugerville is officially incorporated as a city.
  9. 1980
    Still a small town of about 750 people.
  10. Today
    Home to roughly 65,000 neighbors and counting — a growing community that still feels like a hometown.
The best part

The people who call it home

In four decades Pflugerville grew nearly a hundredfold — from a 750-person farm town to a city of about 65,000. What hasn’t changed is the welcome.

A true melting pot

What began as one German family is now one of the most diverse communities in the Austin area — neighbors from every background, raising families side by side.

Still a festival town

From Deutschen Pfest to summer nights at Gilleland Park, Pflugerville keeps its German roots alive with food, music, and a whole lot of pfun.

Room to roam

Miles of hike-and-bike trails, Lake Pflugerville, and growing parks make it easy to get outside — the prairie Henry Pfluger farmed is now everybody’s backyard.
